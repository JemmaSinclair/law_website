# TITLE 6. OF CRIMES AGAINST PUBLIC HEALTH AND SAFETY
## (6)01. Possession Of A Controlled Substance

### 1.01 Definitions:

(a) Possession of a Controlled Substance with Intent to Sell: The act of knowingly and willfully having in one's possession a substance regulated and classified as a controlled substance under state law, without a valid prescription or legal authorization, with the intent to sell or distribute.

(b) Controlled Substance: Any substance, excluding marijuana, the possession of which is regulated by state law due to its potential for abuse, dependency, and impact on public health. Controlled substances include but are not limited to:

		(i) Opioids (e.g., heroin, oxycodone)

		(ii) Stimulants (e.g., methamphetamine, cocaine)

		(iii) Hallucinogens (e.g., LSD, psilocybin mushrooms)

		(iv) Certain prescription medications without a valid prescription

### 1.02 Prohibited Conduct:

(a) Prohibited Drugs: Possession of substances listed as controlled under state law, excluding marijuana, with the intent to sell or distribute is prohibited.

(b) Exceptions for Marijuana: The possession of marijuana for personal use, by individuals of legal age, in accordance with state regulations, is not considered a prohibited conduct under this section.

### 1.03 Penalty:

(a) Misdemeanor or Felony Offense - Possession with Intent to Sell: Possession of a controlled substance with the intent to sell may be deemed a misdemeanor or felony offense, depending on the type and quantity of the substance, punishable by fines, probation, imprisonment, or a combination of these, as determined by the severity and circumstances of the offense.

(b) Thresholds for Misdemeanor and Felony:

		(i) Possession of a controlled substance with the intent to sell, involving a quantity for personal use, may be considered a misdemeanor offense.

		(ii) Possession of a controlled substance with the intent to sell, involving quantities indicative of distribution or trafficking, may be considered a felony offense.

(c) Minimum Penalties:

		(i) For misdemeanor offenses, a minimum term of 30 days of probation or a fine of $1,000 shall be imposed for a first-time offense.

		(ii) For felony offenses, a minimum term of 1 year of imprisonment and a fine of $5,000 shall be imposed for a first-time offense.

		(iii) Subsequent offenses may result in enhanced penalties, including longer imprisonment terms and higher fines.

### 1.04 Reporting and Investigation:

(a) Duty to Report: Any person who becomes aware of possession of prohibited drugs with the intent to sell shall promptly report the matter to law enforcement authorities.

(b) Law Enforcement Response: Law enforcement agencies shall promptly and thoroughly investigate reported cases of possession of prohibited drugs with the intent to sell, taking appropriate action to address public health concerns and enforce drug regulations.

### 1.05 Affirmative Defenses:

(a) Valid Prescription: Possession of a prohibited drug with the intent to sell pursuant to a valid prescription from a licensed medical professional may be considered an affirmative defense.

(b) Legal Authorization: Evidence of legal authorization to possess the prohibited drug with the intent to sell, such as for research or medical purposes, may be considered an affirmative defense.

## (6)02. Possession Of Drug Paraphernalia

### 2.01 Definitions:

(a) Possession of Drug Paraphernalia: The act of knowingly and willfully having in one's possession any equipment, product, or material used, intended for use, or designed for use in the planting, cultivating, growing, harvesting, manufacturing, compounding, converting, producing, processing, preparing, testing, analyzing, packaging, repackaging, storing, containing, concealing, injecting, ingesting, inhaling, or otherwise introducing into the human body a controlled substance in violation of state law.

(b) Drug Paraphernalia: Any equipment, product, or material, excluding items used for the lawful administration or consumption of marijuana, that is intended or designed for use in connection with the planting, cultivating, growing, harvesting, manufacturing, compounding, converting, producing, processing, preparing, testing, analyzing, packaging, repackaging, storing, containing, concealing, injecting, ingesting, inhaling, or otherwise introducing into the human body a controlled substance.

### 2.02 Prohibited Conduct:

(a) Possession of Drug Paraphernalia: No person shall engage in the act of possessing drug paraphernalia, as defined in Section 2.01(a), with the intent to use it for the planting, cultivating, growing, harvesting, manufacturing, compounding, converting, producing, processing, preparing, testing, analyzing, packaging, repackaging, storing, containing, concealing, injecting, ingesting, inhaling, or otherwise introducing into the human body a controlled substance in violation of state law.

### 2.03 Penalty:

(a) Misdemeanor Offense - Possession of Drug Paraphernalia: Possession of drug paraphernalia shall be deemed a misdemeanor offense, punishable by fines, probation, or imprisonment, as determined by the severity and circumstances of the offense.

(b) Minimum Penalties:

		(i) A minimum term of 30 days of probation or a fine of $500 shall be imposed for a first-time offense of possession of drug paraphernalia.

		(ii) A minimum term of 60 days of probation or a fine of $1,000 shall be imposed for subsequent offenses within a 12-month period.

		(iii) Subsequent offenses may result in enhanced penalties, including longer probation terms or higher fines.

### 2.04 Reporting and Investigation:

(a) Duty to Report: Any person who becomes aware of possession of drug paraphernalia shall promptly report the matter to law enforcement authorities.

(b) Law Enforcement Response: Law enforcement agencies shall promptly and thoroughly investigate reported cases of possession of drug paraphernalia, taking appropriate action to enforce drug regulations and prevent substance abuse.

### 2.05 Affirmative Defenses:

(a) Lawful Purpose: Evidence that the possession of drug paraphernalia was for a lawful purpose, such as medical or scientific use, may be considered an affirmative defense.

(b) Legal Authorization: Evidence of legal authorization to possess drug paraphernalia, such as for research or medical purposes, may be considered an affirmative defense.

## (6)03. Maintaining A Place For The Purpose Of Distribution

### 3.01 Definitions:

(a) Maintaining a Place for the Purpose of Distribution: The act of knowingly and willfully owning, leasing, occupying, or controlling any property with the intent to use it for the planting, cultivating, growing, harvesting, manufacturing, compounding, converting, producing, processing, preparing, testing, analyzing, packaging, repackaging, storing, containing, concealing, injecting, ingesting, inhaling, or otherwise distributing controlled substances in violation of state law.

(b) Controlled Substance: Any substance, excluding marijuana, the possession of which is regulated by state law due to its potential for abuse, dependency, and impact on public health.

### 3.02 Prohibited Conduct:

(a) Maintaining a Place for the Purpose of Distribution: No person shall engage in the act of maintaining a place for the purpose of distribution, as defined in Section 3.01(a), by knowingly and willfully owning, leasing, occupying, or controlling any property with the intent to use it for the planting, cultivating, growing, harvesting, manufacturing, compounding, converting, producing, processing, preparing, testing, analyzing, packaging, repackaging, storing, containing, concealing, injecting, ingesting, inhaling, or otherwise distributing controlled substances in violation of state law.

### 3.03 Penalty:

(a) Felony Offense - Maintaining a Place for the Purpose of Distribution: Maintaining a place for the purpose of distribution shall be deemed a felony offense, punishable by fines, probation, imprisonment, or a combination of these, as determined by the severity and circumstances of the offense.

(b) Minimum Penalties:

		(i) A minimum term of 1 year of imprisonment and a fine of $5,000 shall be imposed for a first-time offense of maintaining a place for the purpose of distribution.

		(ii) Subsequent offenses may result in enhanced penalties, including longer imprisonment terms and higher fines.

### 3.04 Reporting and Investigation:

(a) Duty to Report: Any person who becomes aware of a place being maintained for the purpose of distribution shall promptly report the matter to law enforcement authorities.

(b) Law Enforcement Response: Law enforcement agencies shall promptly and thoroughly investigate reported cases of maintaining a place for the purpose of distribution, taking appropriate action to address public health concerns and enforce drug regulations.

### 3.05 Affirmative Defenses:

(a) Lack of Knowledge: Lack of knowledge about the illegal activities occurring on the property may be considered an affirmative defense.

(b) Legitimate Purpose: Evidence that the property was being used for a legitimate purpose, such as a residence or business unrelated to drug distribution, may be considered an affirmative defense.

## (6)04. Manufacture Of A Controlled Substance

### 4.01 Definitions:

(a) Manufacture of a Controlled Substance: The act of knowingly and willfully engaging in the planting, cultivating, growing, harvesting, manufacturing, compounding, converting, producing, processing, or preparing any controlled substance, excluding marijuana, in violation of state law.

(b) Controlled Substance: Any substance, excluding marijuana, the possession of which is regulated by state law due to its potential for abuse, dependency, and impact on public health.

### 4.02 Prohibited Conduct:

(a) Manufacture of a Controlled Substance: No person shall engage in the act of manufacturing a controlled substance, as defined in Section 4.01(a), by knowingly and willfully planting, cultivating, growing, harvesting, manufacturing, compounding, converting, producing, processing, or preparing any controlled substance, excluding marijuana, in violation of state law.

### 4.03 Penalty:

(a) Felony Offense - Manufacture of a Controlled Substance: Manufacture of a controlled substance shall be deemed a felony offense, punishable by fines, probation, imprisonment, or a combination of these, as determined by the severity and circumstances of the offense.

(b) Minimum Penalties:

		(i) A minimum term of 2 years of imprisonment and a fine of $10,000 shall be imposed for a first-time offense of manufacturing a controlled substance.

		(ii) Subsequent offenses may result in enhanced penalties, including longer imprisonment terms and higher fines.

### 4.04 Reporting and Investigation:

(a) Duty to Report: Any person who becomes aware of the manufacture of a controlled substance shall promptly report the matter to law enforcement authorities.

(b) Law Enforcement Response: Law enforcement agencies shall promptly and thoroughly investigate reported cases of the manufacture of a controlled substance, taking appropriate action to address public health concerns and enforce drug regulations.

### 4.05 Affirmative Defenses:

(a) Legal Authorization: Evidence of legal authorization to engage in activities related to controlled substances, such as for research or medical purposes, may be considered an affirmative defense.

(b) Lack of Knowledge: Lack of knowledge about the illegal manufacturing activities may be considered an affirmative defense.

## (6)05. Sale Of A Controlled Substance

### 5.01 Definitions:

(a) Sale of a Controlled Substance: The act of knowingly and willfully transferring, delivering, dispensing, or distributing any controlled substance, excluding marijuana, to another person in violation of state law.

(b) Controlled Substance: Any substance, excluding marijuana, the possession of which is regulated by state law due to its potential for abuse, dependency, and impact on public health.

### 5.02 Prohibited Conduct:

(a) Sale of a Controlled Substance: No person shall engage in the act of selling a controlled substance, as defined in Section 5.01(a), by knowingly and willfully transferring, delivering, dispensing, or distributing any controlled substance, excluding marijuana, to another person in violation of state law.

### 5.03 Penalty:

(a) Felony Offense - Sale of a Controlled Substance: Sale of a controlled substance shall be deemed a felony offense, punishable by fines, probation, imprisonment, or a combination of these, as determined by the severity and circumstances of the offense.

(b) Minimum Penalties:

		(i) A minimum term of 2 years of imprisonment and a fine of $10,000 shall be imposed for a first-time offense of selling a controlled substance.

		(ii) Subsequent offenses may result in enhanced penalties, including longer imprisonment terms and higher fines.

### 5.04 Reporting and Investigation:

(a) Duty to Report: Any person who becomes aware of the sale of a controlled substance shall promptly report the matter to law enforcement authorities.

(b) Law Enforcement Response: Law enforcement agencies shall promptly and thoroughly investigate reported cases of the sale of a controlled substance, taking appropriate action to address public health concerns and enforce drug regulations.

### 5.05 Affirmative Defenses:

(a) Legal Authorization: Evidence of legal authorization to engage in activities related to controlled substances, such as for medical or pharmaceutical purposes, may be considered an affirmative defense.

(b) Lack of Knowledge: Lack of knowledge about the illegal sale activities may be considered an affirmative defense.

## (6)06. Public Intoxication

### 6.01 Definitions:

(a) Public Intoxication: The act of being under the influence of alcohol, controlled substances, or any intoxicating substance to a degree that it poses a danger to oneself, others, or property, while in a public place.

(b) Public Place: Any location that is accessible to the general public, including but not limited to streets, sidewalks, parks, public transportation, and establishments open to the public.

### 6.02 Prohibited Conduct:

(a) Public Intoxication: No person shall engage in the act of public intoxication, as defined in Section 6.01(a), by being under the influence of alcohol, controlled substances, or any intoxicating substance to a degree that it poses a danger to oneself, others, or property, while in a public place.

### 6.03 Penalty:

(a) Misdemeanor Offense - Public Intoxication: Public intoxication shall be deemed a misdemeanor offense, punishable by fines, probation, or other appropriate penalties, as determined by the severity and circumstances of the offense.

(b) Minimum Penalties:

		(i) A minimum fine of $100 shall be imposed for a first-time offense of public intoxication.

		(ii) Subsequent offenses may result in increased fines or other penalties, as determined by the court.

### 6.04 Reporting and Investigation:

(a) Duty to Report: Any person who becomes aware of an individual engaging in public intoxication shall have the option to report the matter to law enforcement authorities.

(b) Law Enforcement Response: Law enforcement agencies shall respond to reports of public intoxication, taking appropriate action to address public safety concerns and provide necessary assistance.

### 6.05 Affirmative Defenses:

(a) Voluntary Intoxication: Evidence that the intoxication resulted from the voluntary consumption of a substance may not be considered an affirmative defense.

(b) Medical Emergency: If the intoxication is the result of a medical emergency or prescribed medication, this may be considered an affirmative defense.

## (6)07. Under The Influence Of A Controlled Substance

### 7.01 Definitions:

(a) Under the Influence of a Controlled Substance: The act of being visibly and demonstrably affected by the consumption, ingestion, or use of a controlled substance, excluding marijuana, to a degree that it impairs the person's physical or mental abilities, judgment, or behavior.

(b) Controlled Substance: Any substance, excluding marijuana, the possession of which is regulated by state law due to its potential for abuse, dependency, and impact on public health.

### 7.02 Prohibited Conduct:

(a) Under the Influence of a Controlled Substance: No person shall engage in the act of being under the influence of a controlled substance, as defined in Section 7.01(a), to a degree that it impairs their physical or mental abilities, judgment, or behavior.

### 7.03 Penalty:

(a) Misdemeanor Offense - Under the Influence of a Controlled Substance: Being under the influence of a controlled substance shall be deemed a misdemeanor offense, punishable by fines, probation, or other appropriate penalties, as determined by the severity and circumstances of the offense.

(b) Minimum Penalties:

		(i) A minimum fine of $200 shall be imposed for a first-time offense of being under the influence of a controlled substance.

		(ii) Subsequent offenses may result in increased fines or other penalties, as determined by the court.

### 7.04 Reporting and Investigation:

(a) Duty to Report: Any person who becomes aware of an individual being under the influence of a controlled substance shall have the option to report the matter to law enforcement authorities.

(b) Law Enforcement Response: Law enforcement agencies shall respond to reports of individuals being under the influence of a controlled substance, taking appropriate action to address public safety concerns and provide necessary assistance.

### 7.05 Affirmative Defenses:

(a) Medical Use: Evidence that the individual is using the controlled substance for medical purposes and has a valid prescription may be considered an affirmative defense.

(b) Lack of Impairment: Evidence that the individual's physical or mental abilities, judgment, or behavior were not impaired by the controlled substance may be considered an affirmative defense.

## (6)08. Littering

### 8.01 Definitions:

(a) Littering: The act of disposing or abandoning waste, rubbish, refuse, or any discarded material, including but not limited to, paper, glass, plastic, or other items, in a public place or on private property without proper authorization for disposal.

### 8.02 Prohibited Conduct:

(a) Littering: No person shall engage in the act of littering, as defined in Section 8.01(a), by disposing of or abandoning waste, rubbish, refuse, or any discarded material in a public place or on private property without proper authorization for disposal.

### 8.03 Penalty:

(a) Misdemeanor Offense - Littering: Littering shall be deemed a misdemeanor offense, punishable by fines, community service, or other appropriate penalties, as determined by the severity and circumstances of the offense.

(b) Minimum Penalties:

		(i) A minimum fine of $50 shall be imposed for a first-time offense of littering.

		(ii) Subsequent offenses may result in increased fines or other penalties, as determined by the court.

### 8.04 Reporting and Investigation:

(a) Duty to Report: Any person who witnesses an act of littering shall have the option to report the matter to law enforcement authorities.

(b) Law Enforcement Response: Law enforcement agencies shall respond to reports of littering, taking appropriate action to address public health concerns and maintain the cleanliness of public spaces.

### 8.05 Affirmative Defenses:

(a) Authorized Disposal: Evidence that the disposal was authorized by the property owner or in accordance with applicable waste disposal regulations may be considered an affirmative defense.

(b) Accidental Spillage: Evidence that the littering occurred as a result of accidental spillage, and reasonable efforts were made to clean or rectify the situation, may be considered an affirmative defense.

## (6)09. Criminal Loitering

### 9.01 Definitions:

(a) Criminal Loitering: The act of remaining or lingering in a public place with the intent to commit an unlawful act, disturb the public peace, or facilitate criminal activity.

### 9.02 Prohibited Conduct:

(a) Criminal Loitering: No person shall engage in the act of criminal loitering, as defined in Section 9.01(a), by remaining or lingering in a public place with the intent to commit an unlawful act, disturb the public peace, or facilitate criminal activity.

### 9.03 Penalty:

(a) Misdemeanor Offense - Criminal Loitering: Criminal loitering shall be deemed a misdemeanor offense, punishable by fines, probation, or other appropriate penalties, as determined by the severity and circumstances of the offense.

(b) Minimum Penalties:

		(i) A minimum fine of $100 shall be imposed for a first-time offense of criminal loitering.

		(ii) Subsequent offenses may result in increased fines or other penalties, as determined by the court.

### 9.04 Reporting and Investigation:

(a) Duty to Report: Any person who observes an act of criminal loitering shall have the option to report the matter to law enforcement authorities.

(b) Law Enforcement Response: Law enforcement agencies shall respond to reports of criminal loitering, taking appropriate action to maintain public safety and prevent criminal activity.

### 9.05 Affirmative Defenses:

(a) Legitimate Purpose: Evidence that the person's presence in the public place had a legitimate purpose and did not involve the intent to commit an unlawful act or disturb the public peace may be considered an affirmative defense.

(b) Lack of Intent: Evidence that the person did not have the intent to commit an unlawful act, disturb the public peace, or facilitate criminal activity may be considered an affirmative defense.
