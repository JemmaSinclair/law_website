TITLE 7. CRIMES AGAINST STATE DEPENDENTS

(7)01. Animal Abuse / Cruelty

1.01 Definitions:

(a) Animal Abuse / Cruelty: The intentional, malicious, or negligent act of causing harm, suffering, or death to an animal, whether domesticated or wild, through physical abuse, neglect, or any other means.

(b) Animal: Any living, non-human creature capable of experiencing pain, suffering, and distress, including but not limited to mammals, birds, reptiles, amphibians, and fish.

1.02 Prohibited Conduct:

(a) Animal Abuse / Cruelty: No person shall engage in the act of animal abuse or cruelty, as defined in Section 1.01(a), by intentionally, maliciously, or negligently causing harm, suffering, or death to an animal.

1.03 Penalty:

(a) Felony Offense - Animal Abuse / Cruelty:

    (i) A minimum fine of $1,000 and 60 days of probation shall be imposed for a first-time offense of felony animal abuse or cruelty.

    (ii) Subsequent felony offenses may result in increased fines, longer probation terms, or imprisonment, as determined by the court.

1.04 Reporting and Investigation:

(a) Duty to Report: Any person who witnesses an act of animal abuse or cruelty shall have the obligation to report the matter to law enforcement authorities or animal welfare organizations.

(b) Law Enforcement Response: Law enforcement agencies shall respond promptly to reports of animal abuse or cruelty, taking appropriate action to protect the welfare of the animals and enforce animal protection laws.

1.05 Affirmative Defenses:

(a) Veterinary Care: Evidence that the actions were taken by a licensed veterinarian in the course of providing necessary medical treatment may be considered an affirmative defense.

(b) Legitimate Agriculture Practices: Evidence that the actions were part of accepted and lawful agricultural practices may be considered an affirmative defense.

(7)02. Sale of Prohibited Products to a Minor

2.01 Definitions:

(a) Sale of Prohibited Products to a Minor: The act of intentionally selling, providing, or distributing prohibited products, including but not limited to drugs, marijuana, and alcohol, to an individual known to be a minor.

(b) Prohibited Products: Any substance or item that is illegal or regulated by state law, including drugs, marijuana, and alcohol, the sale of which is restricted based on age.

(c) Minor: An individual who is under the legal age of 21 for purchasing or consuming regulated products.

2.02 Prohibited Conduct:

(a) Sale of Prohibited Products to a Minor: No person shall engage in the act of selling, providing, or distributing prohibited products, as defined in Section 2.01(b), to an individual known to be a minor.

2.03 Penalty:

(a) Misdemeanor Offense - Sale of Prohibited Products to a Minor:

    (i) A first-time offense of selling prohibited products to a minor shall be deemed a misdemeanor, punishable by fines, probation, or imprisonment, as determined by the court.

    (ii) A minimum fine of $1,000 and 30 days of probation shall be imposed for a first-time misdemeanor offense of selling prohibited products to a minor.

(b) Felony Offense - Sale of Prohibited Products to a Minor:

        (i) Subsequent offenses shall be deemed felony offenses, resulting in increased fines, longer probation terms, or imprisonment, as determined by the court.

        (ii) A first-time felony offense of selling prohibited products to a minor may result in a minimum fine of $5,000 and 90 days of probation.

        (iii) Subsequent felony offenses may result in higher fines, longer probation terms, or imprisonment, as determined by the court.

2.04 Reporting and Investigation:

(a) Duty to Report: Any person who becomes aware of the sale of prohibited products to a minor shall promptly report the matter to law enforcement authorities.

(b) Law Enforcement Response: Law enforcement agencies shall respond promptly to reports of the sale of prohibited products to a minor, taking appropriate action to enforce regulations and protect minors from harmful substances.

2.05 Affirmative Defenses:

(a) Age Verification: Evidence that the seller made reasonable efforts to verify the age of the individual and believed them to be of legal age may be considered an affirmative defense.

(b) Lack of Knowledge: Evidence that the seller had no knowledge or reason to believe that the individual was a minor may be considered an affirmative defense.

(7)03. Child Abuse

3.01 Definitions:

(a) Child Abuse: Any act or failure to act that results in harm, potential harm, or threat of harm to a child's health, safety, or well-being.

(b) Child Negligence: Any act or failure to act that results in harm, potential harm, or threat of harm to a child's health, safety, or well-being, resulting from the parent/guardian's negligence.

(c) Child: An individual under the age of 18.

(d) Guardian: Any persons with legal authority over a child, regardless of parental status.

3.02 Prohibited Conduct:

(a) Child Abuse: No person shall engage in any act or omission that constitutes child abuse, as defined in Section 3.01(a).

(b) Child Negligence: No person shall engage in any act or ommission that constitutes child negligence, as defined in Section 3.01(b)

3.03 Penalty:

(a) Acts of Child Abuse shall be charged with a Felony, with minimum of 200 days of imprisonment and a 10,000 dollar fine.

    (i) Guardians found guilty of Child Abuse will have their parental rights terminated.  Courts may determine reinstatement of rights pending appeal.

(b) Acts of Child Negligence shall be charged with a Misdemeanor, with a minimum of 120 days of imprisonment and a 2,000 dollar fine.

    (i) Guardians found guilty of Child Negligence will have their parental rights reviewed by a court, and possibly terminated..  Courts may determine reinstatement of rights pending appeal.

3.04 Reporting and Investigation:

(a) Duty to Report: Any person who becomes aware of possession of prohibited drugs with the intent to sell shall promptly report the matter to law enforcement authorities.

(b) Law Enforcement Response: Law enforcement agencies shall promptly and thoroughly investigate reported cases of possession of prohibited drugs with the intent to sell, taking appropriate action to address public health concerns and enforce drug regulations.

3.05 Affirmative Defenses:

(a) Valid Prescription: Possession of a prohibited drug with the intent to sell pursuant to a valid prescription from a licensed medical professional may be considered an affirmative defense.

(b) Legal Authorization: Evidence of legal authorization to possess the prohibited drug with the intent to sell, such as for research or medical purposes, may be considered an affirmative defense.
